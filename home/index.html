efficient spiking transformer enabled by partial information

Team Leader
  • Implemented self-attention mechanism in SNNs to leverage both self-attention capability and biological properties of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs).
  • Inspired by the missing information mechanisms in the nervous system, I proposed an efficient spiking Transformer (EST) framework enabled by partial information.
  • Our EST can outperform the state-of-the-art SNN model in terms of accuracy and the number of time steps on both Cifar-10/100 and ImageNet datasets. In particular, the proposed EST model achieves 78.48% top-1 accuracy on the ImageNet dataset with only 16 time steps.

Multi-density DBSCAN clustering

Team Leader
  • Improved the existing DBSCAN parameter adaptive algorithm so that it can adapt two parameters at the same time and speed up this adaptive process.
  • Proposed a new multi-density DBSCAN(AMD-DBSCAN) algorithm, which adapts multiple pairs of parameters for multi-density datasets, so that the algorithm can achieve excellent performance. The experimental results show that our AMD-DBSCAN improves accuracy by 24.7% on average over the state-of-the-art algorithm on Multi-density datasets of extremely variable density, while having no performance loss in Single-density scenarios.
  • As the first author, AMD-DBSCAN: An Adaptive Multi-density DBSCAN for datasets of extremely variable density is published in DSAA2022(CCF-C).
